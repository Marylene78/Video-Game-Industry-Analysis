 🎮 Video Game Industry Analysis: From Blockbusters to "Double-Dipping"
📌 Project Overview
This project explores a comprehensive dataset of video game sales, critic scores, and platform distributions to uncover the drivers of commercial success in the gaming industry. Using Power BI, I transformed raw industry data into actionable insights regarding publisher dominance, genre trends, and the relationship between critical acclaim and market performance.

📊 Key Insights & Results
1. The "Platform Double-Dip" Effect
Analyzed how publishers maximize revenue by re-releasing titles across multiple generations (e.g., GTA V on PS3, PS4, and X360).

Insight: Re-releasing on newer hardware significantly extends the lifecycle and total revenue of a single title.

2. Critic Score vs. Commercial Success
Explored the correlation between professional reviews and sales volume using scatter plot analysis.

Result: A high critic score strongly correlates with higher sales, though "Blockbuster Hits" occasionally defy low scores through brand power.

3. Publisher Market Dominance
Identified the "market makers" leading the industry in total revenue.

Result: Activision emerged as a leader with total sales of 722.77M and a steady average critic score of 7.26.

4. Genre Popularity Trends
Tracked the evolution of player preferences over time.

Result: The Action genre dominated the market in 2009, while Action-Adventure took the lead in 2019, showing a shift toward narrative-driven experiences.

5. The "Console Wars" Revenue Split
Evaluated which hardware platforms provided the most profitable ecosystem for developers.

Result: The PS2 remains the historic king of revenue in this dataset, with a massive sum of 1025.38M in total sales.

6. Developer Efficiency & Output
Analyzed which specific studios have the "Midas Touch"—producing the highest average sales per title.

Result: EA Canada identified as a top-performing developer in terms of consistent output and efficiency.

7. The "Golden Year" Analysis
Identified the most lucrative year in gaming history for this dataset.

Result: 2008 was the peak "Golden Year," recording the highest total gaming sales across all platforms.

🛠️ Tools Used
Power BI: Data visualization, DAX measures, and interactive dashboarding.

Power Query: Data cleaning and transformation (ETL).

Data Modeling: Establishing relationships between Publishers, Platforms, and Titles.
